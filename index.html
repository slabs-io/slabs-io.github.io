Still a bit confused, what's slabs?

Ok, in non marketing language, Slabs is a hosted web application that you can use to track how data changes over time. It supports social media platforms like twitter & facebook, it can scrape web pages for data, it can even track things like the weather or traffic in a certain area. It is completely modular and uses a simple drag & drop system. The user connects these modules, or ‘slabs’, together to form a network, or ‘experiment’. The user then starts the experiment and Slabs begins to track the data. The modules can be connected together in many different ways allowing the user to get exactly the data comparisons they want without any noise. A continuous stream of new slabs will be added to the service regularly, to provide extra data sources and visualisations.

I am a business what can I use it for?

You will be able to use slabs for tracking the performance of things like social media campaigns and online sales. It can also be used for things like tracking what the competition are doing online and how your company compares. It can be integrated into their standard website tracking to see the effect that other online events have on their main website.

I am just a dude that wants data, can I use it?

Absolutely. There are loads of opportunities in slabs for journalists, researchers or hobbyists. The joy of slabs is being creative, try comparing really disparate data sets, we have seen some pretty surprising stuff.
